The fix isn't hiring "better AEs." Why this is #2 and not #1: If you fix pipeline quality but Anuraag is still on every call, you're just creating more meetings for a bottlenecked founder. What I Don't Know Yet

Open Questions
& How They Change the Plan

A GTM architect doesn't pretend to have all the answers before Day 1. What separates a good diagnosis from a bad one is knowing exactly which unknowns are load-bearing. These questions fall into two categories: plan-altering (wrong answer = completely different 90-day strategy) and plan-tuning (answer changes a lever, not the direction).

🚨 Plan-Altering — Wrong Answer = Different Strategy
Q1

Are we supply-constrained or demand-constrained?

The most important unknown. If delivery can't keep up with POVs, I should not open the top of funnel — I'd be creating chaos, not revenue. If delivery has capacity sitting unused, top-of-funnel investment has an outsized ROI.

If Supply-Constrained

90-day priority flips entirely. Days 0–60 are 100% focused on delivery efficiency: POV playbook standardization, AI-powered POV automation, SE capacity modeling, reducing POV cycle time by 30%. Do not invest in SDR expansion, outbound, or content until the machine can absorb more deals. Opening pipeline into a constrained delivery org just kills the 70% close rate — the one thing that's working.

If Demand-Constrained

Top-of-funnel is the #1 investment. SDR qualification criteria, ICP definition, outbound engine, content/YouTube strategy — these all move up the priority ladder. The delivery machine has capacity sitting idle. Every incremental qualified deal that reaches POV has a 70% chance of closing. The ROI on pipeline investment is exceptionally high.

Why this is plan-altering, not plan-tuning: Getting this wrong means investing in the wrong half of the funnel for 90 days. That's a quarter of runway wasted. I need Nikunj's honest answer on Wednesday before I finalize the Thursday presentation order.
Q2

What is the actual POV cycle time, and is it getting longer?

If a POV takes 60 days on average, the math is very different from a 90-day or 120-day average. A lengthening POV cycle is a specific diagnostic signal — it means either delivery is stretched (supply constraint) or POVs are turning into science projects (qualification failure). The trend matters more than the snapshot.

If 90+ Days and Lengthening

Delivery efficiency is the bottleneck. Priority 1 becomes the POV Factory: standardized stage exits, milestone tracking, AI automation of status updates, and a hard rule that POVs without a committed executive readout date get paused. A 60-day POV cap with enforcement gates.

If 45–60 Days and Stable

POV cycle is healthy — focus is upstream. The problem isn't how long POVs take, it's that not enough deals reach POV. Pipeline qualification and TOFU investment moves up the priority list. Delivery is not the bottleneck.

Q3

Is there a formal ICP definition today?

If TrueFoundry has a written, agreed-upon ICP with scoring criteria — I'm tuning it. If it's a shared "vibe" between founders and AEs but nothing documented — I'm building it from scratch in Week 1. The difference is 4 weeks of timeline.

If No Formal ICP Exists

Week 1 deliverable becomes the ICP document. Interview every AE and SDR on what their best-fit customers look like. Pull firmographic data on all 46 customers. Map the patterns — team size, cloud provider, ML models in production, industry vertical. Build the scoring card before touching pipeline tactics.

If ICP Exists but Isn't Enforced

Enforcement is the fix, not the document. The issue is SDRs booking meetings that don't meet the criteria — either because the incentive structure rewards volume over quality, or because they don't understand the ICP deeply enough to apply it. Fix is comp + coaching, not another document.
