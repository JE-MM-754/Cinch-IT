Strategic Architecture

The Big 3, In Order

1
The Opportunity, Address First

Build the Sales System That Scales Beyond Founders

  • All three founders touch every deal, and the 70% POV close rate proves it's working. The goal isn't disruption, it's transition. Layer a scalable system underneath so the close rate stays, but growth isn't capped by founder hours. Founders deploy where they create asymmetric value; AEs own the repeatable motion.
  • Anuraag alone: ~540 hrs/yr at 16% win rate, ~27% of a co-founder's working hours in the early-stage funnel. This transition needs to be handled carefully so buyers don't notice a drop in quality, they notice a step up in responsiveness.
  • What I'd do: Build a pitch any technically-trained AE can execute. Founders become the strategic knockout punch, reserved for $250K+ accounts and CTO-level close conversations.
2
Pipeline Quality

Move More of the Right Deals to POV

  • Two root causes: unqualified deals entering the funnel; AEs leading with product before establishing pain. No quantified pain = no urgency = "send me more info."
  • What I'd do: MEDDPICC as shared qualification language and a POV Prescription Playbook. Every discovery call ends with a committed next step or a clean pass.
3
Revenue Architecture

Premium Pricing and a Connected Buyer Journey

  • $125K ACV is 40-60% below market. Databricks, Azure ML, Bedrock print $200-350K for narrower value props. Better discovery gets to the number the product actually justifies.
  • Target: $200K ACV for enterprise accounts. At 70% POV close, $200K avg vs $125K = 60% revenue lift without a single additional deal closed.
  • Marketing, SDR, and AE are three disconnected functions. AEs spend the first 20 minutes educating buyers that content should have pre-qualified.
  • What I'd do: One continuous buyer journey. Content pre-qualifies. SDRs score against ICP. AEs inherit context and use discovery time on pain, not education.

The 4 Demo Moments That Actually Win Deals

Move This to Minute 10

Gated MCP playground demo, the moment that generates the most buyer questions currently happens 80% through a 60-min call. That is the opening act, not the finale.

Universal Pain Frame

"You have 70-80 MCP servers in your environment right now, and that number will double by Q3. No visibility on cost, data exposure, or what happens when a model provider goes down." This opens the governance conversation before a single feature is mentioned.

Unlock Finance Budget, Lead with Compliance for FSI

For FSI and healthcare buyers: security and guardrails come first. Regulated industry buyers need to hear compliance before they hear capability. Cost chargebacks and team-level attribution follow, turns an IT eval into a CFO conversation.

Proactive Trust-Builder

"No TrueFoundry SDK, you can leave whenever you want." Remove the most common late-stage procurement objection in call 1, not in response to a blocker in month 3.

Coaching Philosophy
Build a team of high-character, high-coachability reps and give them a system worth running. Coach to the gaps every week. The playbook is the standard, not the manager's personal style. Everyone measured the same: MEDDPICC score, pipeline coverage, Stage 1 to POV rate, and POV close rate. People who can't clear certification after a real shot get replaced, quickly and fairly.
