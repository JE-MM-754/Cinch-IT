## Primary ICP: The "Sweet Spot" SMB

### Firmographics
- **Employee count:** 10-150 employees
- **Revenue:** $1M-$50M annual
- **Geography:** Greater Boston metro — within 45 min of Woburn, Newton, or Framingham offices
  - Core: Woburn, Newton, Framingham, Worcester, Waltham, Burlington, Lexington, Natick, Marlborough, Wellesley
  - Extended: Cambridge, Somerville, Boston (metro), Lowell, Brockton, Providence RI (stretch)
- **Industry verticals (high fit):**
  - Healthcare / dental / medical practices (HIPAA = instant need)
  - Legal firms (sensitive client data, compliance)
  - Financial services / accounting firms (PCI, SOX)
  - Manufacturing (operational tech + IT convergence)
  - Professional services (consulting, engineering, architecture)
  - Nonprofit / education (budget-conscious, underserved by MSPs)
  - Real estate / property management (distributed offices)
- **Industry exclusions:** Enterprise (500+ employees), government (long sales cycles), retail chains (low margin)

### Technographics
- No dedicated IT staff OR one overwhelmed "IT person"
- Running on-prem servers or aging infrastructure
- Using consumer-grade security (no EDR, no MFA, basic antivirus)
- Cloud migration incomplete (hybrid mess)
- No business continuity / disaster recovery plan
- Windows 10 machines approaching EOL (Oct 2025 — already past!)

### Behavioral Signals
- Recently posted job for IT Manager / IT Director / Help Desk (trying to hire in-house)
- Searching for "IT support [city]" or "managed IT services"
- Current MSP contract expiring (annual renewal cycle)
- Recent growth: new hires, new office, acquired a company
- Compliance audit coming up or recently failed one
- Had a security incident or data breach

### Psychographics
- Owner/operator who views IT as a cost center (wants it off their plate)
- Values reliability and responsiveness over cheapest price
- Frustrated with current break/fix or unresponsive IT vendor
- Doesn't speak tech — needs a partner who translates to business language
- Risk-averse about cybersecurity but doesn't know what they don't know

---

## Decision Makers
- **Primary:** Owner, CEO, President, Managing Partner
- **Secondary:** Office Manager, COO, CFO (at larger SMBs)
- **Influencer:** The "accidental IT person" (the employee who fixes the printer — they'll champion outsourcing)

## Buying Triggers (Why They Switch Now)
1. Current IT provider is slow/unresponsive
2. Had a security scare (ransomware attempt, phishing, data loss)
3. Compliance deadline approaching
4. Growing and current setup can't scale
5. Key employee who "handled IT" left the company
6. Cyber insurance application denied or premiums spiked
7. Moving offices and need network setup
8. New leadership wants to modernize

## Disqualifiers
- Already has competent in-house IT team (5+ IT staff)
- Enterprise (500+ employees) — not Cinch IT's market
- Government / heavily regulated entity with long procurement cycles
- Extremely price-sensitive / wants cheapest option (bad fit for quality MSP)
- Located outside serviceable geography
