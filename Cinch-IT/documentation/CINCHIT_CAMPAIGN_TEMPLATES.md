## Response Handling Scripts

### When They Say "We're Happy With Current Provider"
"That's great to hear! Good IT support is so important. I'm curious - what made you choose them? And how long have you been working together?

The reason I ask is that I've found businesses often don't realize they could be getting more value from their IT investment. Would you be open to a quick benchmarking conversation? No sales pitch - just want to make sure [Company Name] is getting the best possible service for what you're paying."

### When They Say "Not Interested"  
"No problem at all - I appreciate the honest response. Can I ask what's working well with your current IT setup? Always curious to learn what businesses are doing right.

Also, would it be helpful if I just sent you a quick IT security checklist? No strings attached - just some things that have saved other companies headaches."

### When They Say "Send Me Information"
"Absolutely! But rather than sending generic information, I'd love to customize it for [Company Name]'s specific situation. 

Can you tell me:
1. How many employees are you supporting?
2. Are you mostly in-office, remote, or hybrid?
3. What's your biggest IT frustration right now?

I can put together something much more relevant with those details. Or if you prefer, we could just knock this out in a quick 15-minute call?"

### When They Show Interest
"Great! I can definitely help with that. Let me ask a few quick questions so I come prepared:

1. How many people are on your team now?
2. What's your biggest IT headache at the moment?
3. Are you looking to fix something specific, or just general optimization?

I have a few slots this week - does [Day] at [Time] work, or would [Alternative Day/Time] be better?"

## Objection Handling

### "We don't have budget for IT changes"
"I totally understand - everyone's watching costs carefully. That's actually why this conversation might be valuable. Most of the businesses I work with find they're overspending on IT without realizing it.

The assessment is free, and if I find ways to save you money, those savings often pay for any improvements. Want to at least see where you stand?"

### "We're too busy right now"
"I get it - everyone's slammed. That's often a sign that IT processes could be streamlined to give you time back.

The assessment takes 15 minutes and might identify ways to automate manual tasks you're doing. Could end up saving you hours each week. Worth a quick look?"

### "We've been burned by IT companies before"
"I'm sorry to hear that - there are definitely some bad actors out there. That's exactly why I start with a free assessment with no obligations. You can see how I work before making any decisions.

What went wrong with the previous company? I'd hate for you to miss out on good IT support because of a bad experience."

### "Call me back in [X months]"
"Fair enough! Can I ask what's happening in [X months] that would make the timing better? Is it budget cycle, project completion, or just general bandwidth?

In the meantime, would it be helpful if I sent you a quick IT security checklist? No follow-up required - just want to make sure [Company Name] is protected while you're focused on other priorities."
